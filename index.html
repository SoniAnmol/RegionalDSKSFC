About the Project

This project extends the DSK-SFC (Dosi–Fagiolo–Roventini / Stock-Flow Consistent) agent-based macroeconomic model to a multi-region setting, integrating climate dynamics, an energy sector, and opinion formation to study how regional extreme weather events shape macroeconomic trajectories and climate policy adoption.

A key question motivating the work is why populations exposed to severe climate events do not consistently shift toward climate action — and how heterogeneous regional exposure interacts with opinion dynamics and economic feedback to produce this outcome.
